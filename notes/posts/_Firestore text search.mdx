For years, whatever new feature we announced for Firestore, the bulk of the responses was some variant of:

> Again, no full-text search

And while I think that text-search is inherently quite different from a database query, I always understood where the commenters and mockers were coming from: it's quite ridiculous that a company that is known for its Search product has not ofference a full-text search API since the downturn of the original App Engine.

Well... we're in 2026, and things are quite different now. 

<PullQuote width="70%">
Firestore now has a full-text search API. In fact, it has multiple!
</PullQuote>

In this post, I'll give an overview of the full-text search options available for Firestore users, and shows some results and performance characteristics. Since this is brand new, I expect there will be a lot of improvements and changes in the coming months, but this is what we have as of this writing.

---

### Initial testing during Cloud Next 2026

I started testing the new search capabilities during Cloud Next 2026, and shared some of my findings on socials. Here's a summary of what I found:

https://x.com/puf/status/2046690939700162577 (thread)

> From my initial testing, the search is case-insensitive - which is what most you'll usually want.
> 
> I haven't found any flag yet to make it case-sensitive though, which I also sometimes want.
---
> It seems:
> 
> * The `like` operation is case-sensitive
> * The `documentMatches` operation is case-insensitive
> 
> One example, because length:
> ```js
> db.pipeline().collection('Test')
>   .where(like(field('textField'), "%Flutter%"))
>   .search({
>     query: documentMatches('flutter')
>   })
> ```
---
> It also looks like the `like()` operation can compare against another field value, something that used to be impossible before it:
> ```js
> // Check if the 'title' field contains the string "guide"
> like(field("title"), field("pattern"));
> ```
